## Overview

This assignment implements an **LSTM-based text generation model** trained on the [Epicurious Recipes dataset](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/hugodarwood/epirecipes) from Kaggle.

The model learns to predict the next word in a recipe sequence, and is then used auto-regressively to generate new recipe text. Two different **temperature values (0.5 and 0.8)** are used to demonstrate the effect of sampling randomness on generated output.

> Reference: [Generative Deep Learning, 2nd Edition — Chapter 5: Autoregressive Models](https://github.com/davidADSP/Generative_Deep_Learning_2nd_Edition/blob/main/notebooks/05_autoregressive/01_lstm/lstm.ipynb)

## Model Architecture

```
Input Layer       (None, None)        — integer token IDs
     ↓
Embedding         (None, None, 100)   — 1,000,000 params
     ↓
LSTM              (None, None, 128)   — 117,248 params
     ↓
Dense + Softmax   (None, None, 10000) — 1,290,000 params
     ↓
Output            Next-word probability distribution (vocab = 10,000)

Total params: 2,407,248 (9.18 MB)
```

## Text Generation Results

Text is generated by:
1. Feeding a seed prompt to the network
2. Predicting the next word using **temperature sampling**
3. Appending the predicted word and repeating

### Temperature = 0.5 (more deterministic)

**Prompt:** `recipe for roasted vegetables | chop 1 /`
> *recipe for roasted vegetables | chop 1 / 2 cup water , and 1 / 4 cup sugar . toss with 1 / 2 cup sugar , and salt . in a large bowl , combine the oil , and 1 tablespoon sugar to a small bowl . add the*

**Prompt:** `recipe for chocolate cake |`
> *recipe for chocolate cake | combine all ingredients in a large bowl and stir in the remaining 1 / 2 teaspoon salt . whisk together the egg whites , and the salt . add the butter and stir until smooth . in a bowl combine the egg yolks , the*

---

### Temperature = 0.8 (more creative / diverse)

**Prompt:** `recipe for roasted vegetables | chop 1 /`
> *recipe for roasted vegetables | chop 1 / 2 cups , cut side down , and tartlets until skins are soft and stir to 1 / 2 - inch - thick slices . put in muffin bowls .*

**Prompt:** `recipe for chocolate cake |`
> *recipe for chocolate cake | put oven rack in middle of oven and preheat oven to 400°f . cook the onion on the tomatoes and 1 teaspoon sliced oil in a large bowl and it . add the garlic , 1 tbsp . oil and cook for medium - low*

---

## Key Observations

- **Temperature 0.5** produces repetitive but structured recipe-like text with consistent fractions and standard culinary phrasing.
- **Temperature 0.8** produces more varied and creative text, but can drift off-topic (e.g., adding onions/garlic to a chocolate cake).
- Lower temperature → model picks the highest-probability word more often → safer, more predictable output.
- Higher temperature → probability distribution is flattened → more diverse but sometimes incoherent output.
